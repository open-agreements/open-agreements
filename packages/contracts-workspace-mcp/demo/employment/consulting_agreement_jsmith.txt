INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR CONSULTING AGREEMENT

This Independent Contractor Consulting Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into
effective as of June 1, 2025 ("Effective Date"), by and between:

TechStartup Inc., a Delaware corporation, with its principal place of business at
1400 Innovation Drive, Suite 200, San Francisco, California 94107 ("Company"), and

James Smith, an individual residing at 891 Maple Street, Portland, Oregon 97205
("Consultant").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company desires to engage Consultant to provide certain machine
learning engineering and data science consulting services; and

WHEREAS, Consultant has the requisite expertise and desires to provide such services
on the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

1. SERVICES

1.1 Scope of Work. Consultant shall provide the following services ("Services"):
  (a) Design and implementation of recommendation engine models for the Company's
      core product;
  (b) Development of data pipelines for model training and evaluation;
  (c) Technical documentation and knowledge transfer to Company's engineering team;
  (d) Participation in weekly technical review meetings (remote attendance
      acceptable).

1.2 Standard of Performance. Consultant shall perform the Services in a
professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards. Consultant
shall devote approximately twenty (20) hours per week to the Services.

2. TERM AND TERMINATION

2.1 Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue
for a period of six (6) months, expiring on November 30, 2025, unless earlier
terminated or extended by mutual written agreement of the parties.

2.2 Early Termination. Either party may terminate this Agreement upon fourteen (14)
days' written notice to the other party for any reason or no reason. Upon
termination, Company shall pay Consultant for all Services performed through the
effective date of termination.

2.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately
upon written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails
to cure such breach within seven (7) days of written notice of the breach.

3. COMPENSATION

3.1 Consulting Fee. Company shall pay Consultant at the rate of Two Hundred Fifty
Dollars ($250.00) per hour for Services rendered.

3.2 Invoicing. Consultant shall submit invoices on a bi-weekly basis, itemizing the
hours worked and a brief description of Services performed. Company shall pay each
invoice within fifteen (15) days of receipt.

3.3 Expenses. Company shall reimburse Consultant for reasonable, pre-approved
out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the Services, provided that
Consultant submits receipts and documentation with each invoice. Travel expenses
require prior written approval from the Company.

3.4 No Benefits. Consultant is an independent contractor and shall not be entitled
to any employee benefits, including but not limited to health insurance, retirement
plans, paid vacation, or workers' compensation.

4. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

4.1 Consultant is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, partner, or
joint venturer of the Company. Consultant shall have no authority to bind the
Company or represent that Consultant is an employee or agent of the Company.

4.2 Consultant shall be solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes
arising from compensation received under this Agreement, including self-employment
taxes. The Company will issue Consultant an IRS Form 1099-NEC for compensation paid.

4.3 Consultant retains the right to determine the manner and means of performing
the Services, including the right to perform services for other clients, provided
that such other engagements do not conflict with Consultant's obligations hereunder.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 Work Product. All work product, deliverables, inventions, discoveries, designs,
code, algorithms, documentation, and materials created by Consultant in the
performance of the Services ("Work Product") shall be the sole and exclusive
property of the Company. Consultant hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all
right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including all intellectual
property rights therein.

5.2 Pre-Existing IP. Consultant retains ownership of any tools, libraries,
frameworks, or methodologies owned by Consultant prior to this Agreement or
developed independently outside the scope of the Services ("Pre-Existing IP").
To the extent any Pre-Existing IP is incorporated into the Work Product, Consultant
hereby grants the Company a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free, worldwide
license to use, modify, and sublicense such Pre-Existing IP solely as part of the
Work Product.

5.3 Moral Rights. To the extent permitted by law, Consultant waives any moral
rights in the Work Product.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Consultant acknowledges that in the course of performing the Services,
Consultant may have access to confidential and proprietary information of the
Company ("Confidential Information"). Consultant shall hold all Confidential
Information in strict confidence and shall not disclose or use such information
except as necessary to perform the Services.

6.2 Upon termination of this Agreement or upon Company's request, Consultant shall
promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information in Consultant's possession.

6.3 The obligations under this Section 6 shall survive the termination of this
Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

7. NON-SOLICITATION

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months following
its termination, Consultant shall not directly or indirectly solicit or hire any
employee of the Company, or encourage any employee to leave the Company's employ.

8. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

8.1 Consultant represents and warrants that: (a) Consultant has the skills,
qualifications, and experience necessary to perform the Services; (b) the Services
and Work Product will not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights;
(c) Consultant is not subject to any agreement that would prevent Consultant from
performing the Services; and (d) Consultant will comply with all applicable laws.

8.2 Company represents and warrants that it has the authority to enter into this
Agreement and to engage Consultant to perform the Services.

9. INDEMNIFICATION

9.1 Consultant shall indemnify and hold harmless the Company from any third-party
claims arising from: (a) Consultant's breach of this Agreement; (b) Consultant's
negligence or willful misconduct; or (c) any claim that the Work Product infringes
third-party intellectual property rights.

9.2 Company shall indemnify and hold harmless Consultant from any third-party
claims arising from Company's use of the Work Product in a manner not contemplated
by this Agreement.

10. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL,
CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES. CONSULTANT'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER
THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID TO CONSULTANT DURING THE THREE
(3) MONTH PERIOD PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

11. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of
the State of Oregon, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Any
disputes shall be resolved in the state or federal courts located in Multnomah
County, Oregon.

12. GENERAL PROVISIONS

12.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between
the parties regarding the subject matter hereof.

12.2 Amendment. This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement signed
by both parties.

12.3 Severability. If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions
shall remain in effect.

12.4 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts.
