INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT AGREEMENT

This Intellectual Property Assignment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of
February 1, 2025 ("Effective Date"), by and between:

TechStartup Inc., a Delaware corporation, with its principal place of business at
1400 Innovation Drive, Suite 200, San Francisco, California 94107 ("Company"), and

The undersigned individual ("Assignor"):

Name: Michael Torres
Address: 2210 Pacific Heights Road, San Francisco, California 94115
Role: Co-Founder and Chief Technology Officer

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Assignor is a co-founder of the Company and has, prior to and since the
Company's incorporation, conceived, developed, and reduced to practice certain
intellectual property relating to the Company's business;

WHEREAS, the Company desires to obtain full ownership of all such intellectual
property to protect its business interests and satisfy the requirements of its
investors; and

WHEREAS, Assignor desires to assign such intellectual property to the Company on
the terms set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, Assignor's
continued employment, and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and
sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Assigned IP" means all intellectual property, inventions, discoveries, works
of authorship, trade secrets, know-how, software, source code, algorithms, designs,
specifications, documentation, data, and other proprietary materials that Assignor
has conceived, created, developed, or reduced to practice, either alone or jointly
with others, that relate to the Company's current or reasonably anticipated
business, products, or services, including without limitation:

  (a) The "Nexus" distributed data processing framework (initial prototype
      developed from September 2024 through January 2025);
  (b) The proprietary machine learning pipeline architecture for real-time
      analytics;
  (c) All associated documentation, specifications, test suites, and technical
      notes;
  (d) The "TechStartup" name, logo, and related branding materials; and
  (e) All patent applications, copyright registrations, and other filings related
      to any of the foregoing.

1.2 "Excluded IP" means only the items listed on Exhibit A attached hereto, which
Assignor represents constitute all intellectual property conceived or developed
prior to Assignor's involvement with the Company and that are not related to the
Company's business.

2. ASSIGNMENT

2.1 Assignment of Rights. Assignor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and
conveys to the Company all of Assignor's right, title, and interest in and to the
Assigned IP, including without limitation all:

  (a) patents and patent applications, including all provisionals, continuations,
      continuations-in-part, divisionals, reissues, and extensions;
  (b) copyrights and copyright registrations;
  (c) trade secrets and confidential information;
  (d) trademarks and service marks;
  (e) all rights to sue for past, present, and future infringement; and
  (f) all other intellectual property rights of any kind, in all jurisdictions
      worldwide.

2.2 Further Assurances. Assignor agrees to execute any additional documents and
take any further actions reasonably requested by the Company to evidence, perfect,
or enforce the assignment of rights hereunder, including executing patent
applications, copyright registrations, and assignments as may be necessary.

2.3 Power of Attorney. Assignor hereby irrevocably appoints the Company and its
officers as Assignor's attorney-in-fact, with full power of substitution, to
execute and file any documents and take any actions necessary to accomplish the
purposes of this Agreement, should Assignor fail or refuse to do so within a
reasonable time after request.

3. CONSIDERATION

3.1 In consideration for the assignment of the Assigned IP, the Company shall:
  (a) continue Assignor's employment as CTO under the existing employment
      agreement dated February 1, 2025;
  (b) maintain Assignor's equity grant of 2,000,000 shares of common stock
      subject to the Company's standard four-year vesting schedule with a
      one-year cliff; and
  (c) pay Assignor a one-time IP assignment fee of Ten Thousand Dollars
      ($10,000.00) within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date.

4. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

4.1 Assignor represents and warrants that:

  (a) Assignor is the sole owner of all right, title, and interest in and to the
      Assigned IP, free and clear of any liens, encumbrances, or claims of any
      third party;
  (b) The Assigned IP does not infringe, misappropriate, or otherwise violate any
      intellectual property or proprietary rights of any third party;
  (c) Assignor has not previously assigned, transferred, licensed, or encumbered
      any rights in the Assigned IP to any third party;
  (d) Assignor has not used any intellectual property belonging to any former
      employer or other third party in the development of the Assigned IP;
  (e) Exhibit A is a complete and accurate list of all Excluded IP; and
  (f) There are no pending or threatened claims or litigation relating to the
      Assigned IP.

5. ONGOING OBLIGATIONS

5.1 Future Inventions. Assignor agrees that during the term of Assignor's
employment with the Company, all inventions, works of authorship, and intellectual
property that Assignor conceives, develops, or reduces to practice that relate to
the Company's business shall be the sole property of the Company and are
automatically assigned to the Company.

5.2 Disclosure. Assignor shall promptly disclose to the Company all inventions and
intellectual property developed during Assignor's employment that may relate to the
Company's business.

5.3 Notice Under California Labor Code Section 2870. Assignor is hereby notified
that this Agreement does not apply to any invention that qualifies fully under
California Labor Code Section 2870, which provides that an employee's invention
is excluded if it was developed entirely on the employee's own time, without
using the employer's equipment or trade secrets, and does not relate to the
employer's business.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Assignor shall maintain the confidentiality of all Assigned IP and all other
confidential information of the Company, and shall not disclose such information
to any third party without the Company's prior written consent.

6.2 This confidentiality obligation shall survive the termination of Assignor's
employment with the Company indefinitely with respect to trade secrets, and for a
period of five (5) years with respect to other Confidential Information.

7. GOVERNING LAW

7.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws
of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

7.2 Any dispute arising out of this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively in the
state or federal courts located in Delaware.

8. MISCELLANEOUS

8.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the
parties with respect to the assignment of the Assigned IP.

8.2 Severability. If any provision is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions
shall continue in full force.

8.3 Binding Effect. This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit
of the parties and their respective heirs, successors, and assigns.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the Effective Date.
