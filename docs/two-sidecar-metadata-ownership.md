# Two-Sidecar Metadata Ownership

Some templates in `content/templates/*/` have a sibling file named
`metadata.legal-context.yaml`. This document explains what it is, who owns it,
and why it exists.

## TL;DR

| File | Owner | Hand-edit? | Regenerated by |
|------|-------|------------|----------------|
| `metadata.yaml` | **open-agreements** (this repo) | Yes — hand-authored | — |
| `metadata.legal-context.yaml` | **legal-context** (sibling repo) | **No — will be overwritten** | `legal-context/scripts/sync_open_agreements.py` |

Filename tells you who owns it. No per-field flags, no memorization.

## Why two files?

Some metadata is pure template structure — field names, types, UI sections,
priority fields, descriptions, license, attribution, category. That's
hand-authored in `metadata.yaml` by template contributors.

Other metadata is derived from legal research — the `default` values lawyers
pick, the `default_value_rationale` explaining why, and the `options` array for
multi-choice fields. That lives in the state-specific practice note at
`legal-context/notes/non-compete/<state>.md` (the `restrictive_covenant_profile`
frontmatter block) and gets synced here by legal-context's
`scripts/sync_open_agreements.py`.

Splitting these into two files keeps ownership obvious — a developer looking at
a changed file knows from the filename whether their edit will survive or be
stomped on next sync.

## What keys may the sidecar set?

Per field entry:

- `name` — must match a field name declared in the sibling `metadata.yaml`
- `default`
- `default_value_rationale`
- `options`
- `display`

Any other top-level key (e.g. `description`, `type`, `section`) would mean
legal-context is claiming ownership of template structure, which it isn't. The
CI lint at `scripts/validate_metadata_sidecar.mjs` enforces this.

## Fill-pipeline merge rule

At fill time, `loadMetadata(templateDir)` in `src/core/metadata.ts` deep-merges
the two files per field:

- Fields in both: sidecar wins for `default`, `default_value_rationale`,
  `options`, `display`. Everything else comes from `metadata.yaml`.
- Fields only in `metadata.yaml`: pass through unchanged (e.g. covenants not
  yet researched by legal-context keep their hand-authored defaults).
- Fields only in the sidecar: **CI failure** — means the template renamed or
  removed a field and the sidecar wasn't regenerated.

## What if I renamed a field in `metadata.yaml` and CI now fails?

Two ways to fix:

1. **Also edit `metadata.legal-context.yaml` in the same PR** — find the entry
   referencing the old name, rename it. Fastest when you know the mapping.

2. **Delete `metadata.legal-context.yaml` in the PR** — the next legal-context
   sync regenerates it with the new name. Simplest when the rename is
   structural and the sync-bot will pick it up on its next run.

Either way, CI gates you until the two files agree.

## What if I edited the sidecar and my edit keeps reverting?

That's by design. Anything in `metadata.legal-context.yaml` is a generated
artifact; the next sync from legal-context will overwrite it. If the content
there is wrong, the fix is in legal-context's practice note (the caveat,
`primary_authority`, or `duration_rule` in the state profile block), not here.
