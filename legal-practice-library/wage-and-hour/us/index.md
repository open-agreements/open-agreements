* [Wage and Hour Law in Michigan](michigan.md) - A question-by-question summary of Michigan wage and hour law, covering the Improved Workforce Opportunity Wage Act minimum wage of $13.73 that rises to $15.00 on January 1, 2027 and is CPI-indexed after that, the weekly-only overtime rule, the absence of any adult meal-or-rest-break mandate, the Payment of Wages and Fringe Benefits Act rules on next-payday final pay backed by an administrative 10% annual penalty rather than an automatic waiting-time penalty, semi-monthly paydays and itemized pay statements, the economic-reality test for worker classification, and a tipped cash wage that rises from 40% of the minimum wage in 2026 to 50% by 2031, which 2025 PA 1 preserved but narrowed rather than eliminating the partial tip credit.
