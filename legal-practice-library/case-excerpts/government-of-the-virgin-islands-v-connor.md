# Government of the Virgin Islands v. Connor

### government-of-the-virgin-islands-v-connor-courts-should-consider-three-e9bbffd4 {#government-of-the-virgin-islands-v-connor-courts-should-consider-three-e9bbffd4}

> courts should consider “three non-dispositive factors” to determine Virgin Islands common law: “(1) whether any Virgin Islands courts have previously adopted a particular rule; (2) the position taken by a majority of courts from other jurisdictions; and (3) most importantly, which approach represents the soundest rule for the Virgin Islands.”

### government-of-the-virgin-islands-v-connor-rather-this-court-has-instructed-that-4ae40edd {#government-of-the-virgin-islands-v-connor-rather-this-court-has-instructed-that-4ae40edd}

> Rather, this Court has instructed that, instead of mechanistically following the Restatements, courts should consider "three non-dispositive factors" to determine Virgin Islands common law: "(1) whether any Virgin Islands courts have previously adopted a particular rule; (2) the position taken by a majority of courts from other jurisdictions; and (3) most importantly, which approach represents the soundest rule for the Virgin Islands."
