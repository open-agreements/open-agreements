# International Cleaning Services (Australia) Pty Ltd v Dmytrenko

*International Cleaning Services (Australia) Pty Ltd v Dmytrenko [2020] SASC 222 (Stanley J).*

Cited authority (case law). Quoted across 1 practice note(s) in the OpenAgreements legal practice library.

## Quoted propositions

### Quote 1 {#q1}

> A stricter view is taken of covenants in restraint of trade in employment contracts than those contained in contracts for the sale of a business.

- **Supports:** `enforceability`
- **Cited by:** [Non-Compete Enforceability in South Australia](../non-compete/australia-south-australia.md)

### Quote 2 {#q2}

> An employer is not entitled to protect itself against mere competition by a former employee, and that employee is entitled to use his or her skill, experience and know-how acquired in the service of the former employer in legitimate competition.

- **Supports:** `legitimate-interest`
- **Cited by:** [Non-Compete Enforceability in South Australia](../non-compete/australia-south-australia.md)
- [Deep link to this passage](<https://www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/cases/sa/SASC/2020/222.html>)

### Quote 3 {#q3}

> At common law a restraint of trade is contrary to public policy and void unless it can be shown that the restraint is, in the particular circumstances of the case, reasonable.

- **Supports:** `enforceability`, `federal-reform`
- **Cited by:** [Non-Compete Enforceability in South Australia](../non-compete/australia-south-australia.md)
